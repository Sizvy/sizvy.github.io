Research

I have a deep interest in the fields of security and privacy, with a particular focus on understanding and countering privacy issues in machine learning algorithms.. During my PhD journey at the University of California, Riverside (UCR), I aim to contribute to this area through rigorous research and exploration.

In the final year of my undergraduate studies, I delved into cloud security, with a specific emphasis on container security. This experience allowed me to build a strong foundation in securing cloud environments, a critical area in today's rapidly evolving technological landscape.

Apart from my PhD research work, very recently I was working on an exciting research project titled "Vein-Based Imaging Approach for Real-Time Dehydration Detection". This project leverages advanced techniques in deep learning and image processing to develop a novel approach for detecting dehydration in real-time using vein imaging. This interdisciplinary work sits at the intersection of health technology, artificial intelligence, and computer vision, reflecting my broad research interests and commitment to impactful, real-world solutions. This paper is currently under review at IMWUT journal.
